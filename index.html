Volunteer

I am currently volunteering at Peter Lougheed Centre's patient care department and UofC's Rehabilitation Centre. Volunteering at these two departmetns provided me with meaningful exposure to patient focused care within two different healthcare settings. From these experiences, I am able to support patients, families and staff, learning the importance of compassion, professionalism and clear communication in healthcare. In these environments, I am able to contribute to the comfort and well being of patients who may be experiencing stress, recovery or other health related challenges. This helps me understand how healthcare teams work together within different systems, and how to create a supportive and respective environment for patients.

After years in these volunteering roles, I gained valuable interpersonal and professional skills that continuously benefit me in future academic and career settings. Through countless interactions, I have strengthened my ability to communicate with empathy, focusing on ensuring patient centered care within sensitive situations and adapting to the needs of different individuals. I have also learned the importnace of active listening, teamwork and maintaining a calm prescence in fast paced care settings. overall, volunteering in these two locations has deepened my appreciation for healthcare service and helped me grow into compassion and responsibility.

Projects

Prior to taking this class, I had no background in coding or computer science, and as a result, each project required me to develop new skills from the ground up. Throughout this process, I demonstrated initiative, persistance and a strong willingness to learn and problem solve when things don't go right. In addition, these projects highlight my curiosity and openness to learning as well as my application of new knowledge in a meaningful manner. Most important of all, they demonstrate my commitment to growth, as I was able to push myself in learning new concepts I am completely unfamiliar with and turn newly acquired concepts into completed work I am proud of.

Goals

My academic goal is to pursue adminsion into physiotherapy school, as I believe that is where my skillset will be best utilized, and combined with my growing interest with healthcare, I my desire is to make a meaningful difference in the lives of others. Through my experiences in school, volunteering and working with people, I developed a strong appreciation for the impact health car eprofessionals have on individual's wellbeing, recovery and quality of life. Physiotherapy is appealing to me because it combines science, movement and patient centered care that allows practitioners to support people throu gh both challenge and progress. Above that, I want to build a career focused on helping as many people as I can through many different means such as improving physical function, supporting their recovery or through simply making a positive difference. When semesters get stressful and I find myself too exhausted to volunteer, this goal is always actively motivating me academically and personally as I work towards a future in healthcare.
